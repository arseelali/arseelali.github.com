Evidence Objections

"Objection your honor, Relevance..."

     Is it relevant?

     Ex. If the topic is the murder and the lawyer starts talking about a TV show.

"Objection your honor, Hearsay..."

     Is the question or answer referencing something said by someone outside of court?

     Ex. I don't really know but I heard Jacob say that he was bad news.

"Objection your honor, Rule 403..."

     Is there any evidence that is trying to sway the jury through extreme feelings or emotions?

     Ex. Take a look at this picture of the deceased. Look how he was brutally stabbed.

"Objection your honor, Improper Character Evidence..."

     Is the question or answer talking about the past or character of a person in a negative way?

     Ex. I want the jury to note that the defendant Mr. Jacob has a past record of not repaying debts and is a generally bad person to friends and colleauges.

"Objection your honor, Improper Opinion..."

     Is the answer an opinion? (Exception of experts)

     Ex. I think Johnny is a bad person becuse he didn't come to my birthday party.

"Objection your honor, Speculation, Lack of Personal Knowledge..."

     Is the response speculatory (does the witness have to guess)? Does the response include something that the witness is not an expert about?

     Ex. Does Johnny have a blue Honda? I think so.

"Objection your honor, Calls for Speculation..."

     Does the QUESTION require the witness to speculate (guess)?

     Ex. Do you think Johnny was insane?

"Objection your honor, Asked and Answered..."

     Has the question already been asked and answered?

     Ex. What is your occupation? I am a Police Officer. According to you, your occupation is an Officer of the Law, correct? Yes, I am.

"Objection your honor, Argumentative..."

     Is the "question" even a question? Is it more like a statement?

     Ex. Johhny is guilty.

"Objection your honor, Badgering..."

     Is the lawyer being hostile or argumentative, or repetitive ot the witness?

     Ex. You yelling at that person means you must be very aggressive?

"Objection your honor, Witness is being non-responsive..."

     Is the witness refusing to answer the question asked?

     Ex. What is your name? [No response]

"Objection your honor, Compound Question..."

     Does the question have more than one question in it?

     Ex. You were at the house at 12:00 PM and at the hotel at 1:00 PM correct?

"Objection your honor, Calls for Narrative..."

     Is the question asking for a narrative from the witness?

     Ex. How do you remember the night?

"Objection your honor, Assumes facts not in the evidence..."

     Is the lawyer assuming facts that have not been submitted into evidence?

     Ex. Johnny was an alcoholic (has not been stated before in evidence).

"Objection your honor, Lacks foundation..."

     Before asking a conclusive question, was there lack of evidence of personal knowledge? Are they qualified enough to answer that question?

     Ex. What's your name? Johnny. What is your occupation? I am a tennis player. How much rubber does a tennis ball have?
